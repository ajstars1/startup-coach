# Where to Play — Market Opportunity Navigator

**Credits:** Marc Gruber & Sharon Tal, *Where to Play* — [wheretoplay.co](https://wheretoplay.co/). Original worksheets available there free (CC BY-NC-SA 4.0); this skill teaches the method and links to the source — get the printable canvases from their site.

**Core definitions:** a **market opportunity = any combination of application + customer**. A market = a group of people and the job they are trying to get done.

## Worksheet 1 — Generate your Market Opportunity Set

1. List the venture's **core abilities / technological elements**, characterized by functions and properties, described **generally — independent of your envisioned product**. (This is why the Navigator works with NO idea yet: start from what you can do.)
2. Ask: which **applications** can these abilities offer? Which **customers** may need each? Zoom in — segment each customer group further.
3. Each application + customer combo goes into the set. Abilities are usually `[FACT]` (the founder knows what they can build); every proposed combo is `[ASSUMPTION]`.

## Worksheet 2 — Evaluate attractiveness (one per opportunity)

Rate each factor Low / Mid / High / Super High — **every rating needs a stated basis** (source URL, interview evidence, or explicitly "provisional [ASSUMPTION]"). Never rate on vibes.

**POTENTIAL** — *Compelling reason to buy* (unmet need; effective solution; better than current solutions) · *Market volume* (current size; expected growth) · *Economic viability* (margins; customers' ability to pay; stickiness)

**CHALLENGE** — *Implementation obstacles* (product development; sales & distribution; funding) · *Time to revenue* (development time; product-to-market gap; sale-cycle length) · *External risks* (competitive threat; 3rd-party dependencies; adoption barriers)

Combine → Overall Potential × Overall Challenge → place on the **Attractiveness Map**: **Gold Mine** (high potential / low challenge) · **Moon Shot** (high/high) · **Quick Win** (low/low) · **Questionable** (low/high).

## Worksheet 3 — Agile Focus Strategy

I. Choose a **Primary Market Opportunity** from the map.
II. Assess other attractive opportunities for relatedness to it:
- **Product relatedness** — shared technology, resources, networks
- **Market relatedness** — shared customer values/benefits, sales channels, word-of-mouth
- **Growth option** — related opportunity that adds value on top of the primary
- **Backup option** — attractive opportunity that does NOT share the primary's major risks (your change-of-direction insurance)

III. **Keep at least one Backup and one Growth option open**; pursue now / keep open / place in storage for everything else (the Agile Focus Dartboard). Focused ≠ single-option: a smart portfolio de-risks the focus.

## Output

The three worksheets filled (markdown tables fine), each rating with its basis label, a recommended primary opportunity stated as **application + customer + why**, the agile-focus portfolio — and crucially: **which UNKNOWNs, if answered differently, would flip the recommendation**. Route those to `/secondary-research` and `/customer-discovery`.

**Fill-in templates:** `TEMPLATES.md` in this skill's folder — copy the structures into your project's `discovery/` and fill with labels.
