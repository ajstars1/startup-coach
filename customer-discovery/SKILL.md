# Customer Discovery & Interviewing

**Method:** Steve Blank & Bob Dorf (Customer Development, *The Startup Owner's Manual*; free course: [How to Build a Startup](https://www.udacity.com/course/how-to-build-a-startup--ep245)) and Giff Constable (*[Talking to Humans](https://www.talkingtohumans.com/)*, [12 Tips](https://giffconstable.com/2010/07/12-tips-for-early-customer-development-interviews/)).

**Foundation:** there are **no facts inside the building** — and discovery is done **by the founders**, never delegated (not to an employee, a survey form, or an AI). This skill PREPARES and DEBRIEFS interviews; only real conversations produce evidence.

## The interview rulebook (Constable's 12 Tips, condensed)

1. **Work to their schedule**, not yours.
2. **Get psyched to hear things you don't want to hear** — the goal is learning, not a sale.
3. **Disarm politeness training** — people won't call your baby ugly; tell them honesty is the only way they can help you.
4. **Start with behavior, not feedback** — their current process in the problem space first; your solution last (if at all). Ask about competitors, even indirect ones, and how they found them.
5. **Open-ended questions only** — never yes/no; never embed the answer you want.
6. **Listen, don't talk** — ~80% silence; don't fill pauses; don't sell.
7. **Encourage but don't influence** — nods and "interesting", nothing that steers.
8. **Follow your nose and drill down** — always chase the "why" behind the "what".
9. **Parrot back to confirm** — they'll correct or refine.
10. **Ask for introductions** — leave every interview with 2–3 names.
11. **Do the paired follow-ups** — one person interviews, one takes notes where possible.
12. **Write notes immediately** — within 30 minutes, before details fade.

**Named failure modes to avoid (Blank):** Death by Demo · Death by PowerPoint · Assuming You Know · Customers Lie · The Distracted Customer · The User, the Buyer & the Saboteur (B2B roles differ — interview them separately) · overweighting one anecdote.

## Interview PREP protocol (what this skill produces before a conversation)

1. **Who:** target archetype + where to find them. Free interview streams founders forget: existing customers at the moment of purchase, **lapsed customers** (information-dense — they chose to leave), referral chains ("who else do you know…?").
2. **Hypotheses under test:** pull 3–5 ASSUMPTIONS from the F/A/U list; for each, the pass/fail signal.
3. **Question guide** (a guide, not a script): behavior-first, past-tense stories ("walk me through the LAST time you…"), the trigger question ("what was happening that day that made you act?"), decider/payer question, price anchored to real past spending — never "would you pay X?". Localize phrasing to the customer's language.
4. **The dual-role trap:** if the meeting is also a sales/service delivery, do the job first, learn second — then ask permission for 5 minutes of questions.
5. **Cadence:** consumer businesses need 50–100 conversations; ~12–15/week is a serious pace. Track weekly counts by face-to-face / phone / video.

## Interview DEBRIEF protocol (when the founder reports back)

1. Capture **what was said/done** (their words, quoted) strictly separate from **interpretation**.
2. Score **commitment signals**: money (paid, pre-ordered), time (gave more of it willingly), introductions (actual names/numbers). "Great idea!" with none of these = politeness, not validation.
3. **Move F/A/U items explicitly**: which assumptions gained evidence, which were contradicted, which new unknowns appeared. n=1 is a direction, never a verdict.
4. Watch for: the founder pitched instead of listened; positives inflated by politeness; a single story treated as a pattern.
5. **Persist the debrief** to `discovery/interviews/<date>-<n>.md` in the project, with gaps (⬜) to close at the next touchpoint.

```
Interview #N — [who], [date], [type], interviewer: [founder]
WHAT THEY SAID/DID (facts only, quoted):
WHAT I THINK IT MEANS (interpretation, separate):
COMMITMENT SIGNALS (money/time/intros):
F/A/U MOVEMENTS (gained evidence / contradicted / new unknowns):
REFERRALS COLLECTED:
DID I PITCH INSTEAD OF LISTEN? (honesty check)
```

*Every question guide is hypotheses in disguise; every debrief is bookkeeping on your beliefs. Nothing here is customer evidence until a real human says it.*
