# Why zero-hallucination coaching

An LLM asked for startup advice will happily produce a TAM figure, three customer personas with names and feelings, and a confident go-to-market plan — none of it anchored to anything. For code, hallucination fails fast: it doesn't compile. For startup strategy, it fails slow: you find out six months and your savings later. That asymmetry is the entire reason this repo exists.

## The design bet

Steve Blank's line — *there are no facts inside the building* — was written about founders. It applies doubly to AI: the model has never stood in your market, met your customer, or watched a launch die. So these skills never let the AI play customer. Its legitimate jobs are:

1. **Bookkeeper of beliefs** — forcing every claim into FACT / ASSUMPTION / UNKNOWN, so the founder always knows what their plan actually stands on.
2. **Researcher** — converting researchable unknowns into sourced facts, with URLs, or admitting failure.
3. **Interview coach** — preparing question guides and debriefing real conversations, per Constable and Blank.
4. **Framework operator** — filling canvases as clearly-labeled hypothesis drafts, faithfully to their authors' methods.
5. **Adversary** — hunting for the founder's (and its own) unlabeled assumptions, including the meta ones like "our silent launch proved no demand".

## Two rules that matter more than the rest

**Evidence has a hierarchy.** A paying transaction > a commitment of time or an introduction > a behavior observed > a story about past behavior > an opinion > a compliment. Skills score evidence accordingly; "great idea!" counts for nothing.

**"Not now" is a success.** These skills are calibrated to never push "pursue" to make the user feel good. A founder who walks away from a weak opportunity with real information got a good outcome — cheaper than the alternative way of learning the same thing.

## Lineage

The frameworks are the published work of Steve Blank & Bob Dorf, Alexander Osterwalder & Yves Pigneur (with Bernarda & Smith), Marc Gruber & Sharon Tal, Giff Constable & Frank Rimalovski, Clayton Christensen, Tony Ulwick, Dave Gray, and Eric Ries — see the README for links. The sequencing and evidence-first drilling are inspired by the incubation programs of the Gopalakrishnan-Deshpande Centre (GDC) at IIT Madras, where these frameworks are taught as one coherent path rather than isolated canvases.
