# Business Model Canvas (BMC) + Market Type + Unit Economics

**Credits:** Alexander Osterwalder & Yves Pigneur — *Business Model Generation* ([strategyzer.com](https://www.strategyzer.com/library/the-business-model-canvas), canvas free). Market types, Get/Keep/Grow, customer development — Steve Blank & Bob Dorf (*The Startup Owner's Manual*; free course: [How to Build a Startup](https://www.udacity.com/course/how-to-build-a-startup--ep245)).

**Blank's framing:** a startup is a **temporary organization searching for a repeatable, scalable business model**. Every block on the canvas is a **hypothesis to test**, not a plan to execute. Fill it in pencil.

## The 9 blocks (each entry labeled F/A/U)

**Value Propositions** (import from `/value-proposition-canvas`) · **Customer Segments** (import from `/jtbd` + `/where-to-play`) · **Channels** · **Customer Relationships** · **Revenue Streams** · **Key Resources** · **Key Activities** · **Key Partners** · **Cost Structure**.

Rules: revenue and cost numbers need sources or `[UNKNOWN + the experiment that measures it]`. Identify the **riskiest block** (early on: usually customer segment or revenue) and propose the cheapest test for it.

## Market Type — decides everything downstream (Blank)

**Existing** (better product, known competitors) · **Resegmented** (niche or low-cost carve-out) · **New** (no existing users — education costs dominate, adoption is slow, time-to-profitability is long) · **Clone** (proven model, new geography). Classify the venture, state the implications for discovery, pricing, and burn — and label the classification `[ASSUMPTION]` until customer behavior confirms it. Misreading a new market as an existing one is a classic startup killer.

## Customer Relationships — Get / Keep / Grow

- **Get:** paid vs earned demand creation; for physical channels, awareness → interest → consideration → purchase; for web, acquisition funnels and viral loops. A launch without a Get plan isn't a launch — it's a coin toss with one flip. Word-of-mouth chains, referral asks, and existing-customer bases are Get channels too (often the cheapest — instrument them).
- **Keep:** loyalty, service quality, switching costs.
- **Grow:** upsell, cross-sell, referrals-by-design.

## Unit economics

- **CAC** (per channel — only measurable from real experiments) and **LTV** (repeat frequency × margin × lifetime). Viability requires LTV meaningfully above CAC; both are `[UNKNOWN]` until measured — never estimate them unlabeled.
- Service businesses: cost-to-serve per order (travel, time, delivery) vs order value — instrument the first deliveries (km, minutes, order value) and check overhead % before scaling or hiring.
- Pricing: anchor to what customers *actually paid* for alternatives (behavioral), not survey answers. Named, visible surcharges beat silently-bundled costs — you learn willingness to pay only when the price is visible.

## Output

The 9-block canvas in markdown (every entry labeled), market-type classification + implications, a Get/Keep/Grow plan with per-channel tracking, the riskiest-block test proposal, and a "Validate next" list. Route interview questions to `/customer-discovery` and desk research to `/secondary-research`.
