# Jobs to be Done (JTBD) + Market Definition

**Credits:** JTBD theory — Clayton Christensen. Job statements / job maps / outcome statements — Tony Ulwick's Outcome-Driven Innovation. Customer-segment discipline — Steve Blank.

**Core definition:** *a market is a group of people and the job they are trying to get done.* Not a product category, not a demographic. People don't want a quarter-inch drill; they hire products to make progress in a circumstance.

## Job Statement — the three pitfalls (check every statement against all three)

1. **Your words, not the customer's.** You may use specialist jargon; the customer doesn't. Ask: *"would a customer use these words to describe their needs / outcomes / experience?"*
2. **The customer is not you.** Don't assume they already know the problem you want to solve or the product you haven't built. The job statement reflects the customer's **current** knowledge and awareness.
3. **Describe the situation, not your solution.** The customer has a bigger perspective; your (future) product merely has a role in it. "Get my stitching done without leaving the house" — not "use my booking app".

Form: **verb + object + context** ("reach the airport on time from home", not "use my taxi app").

## The sequence

1. **Segment by job, not demographics** — people sharing the same job + circumstances. Identify 2–4 **archetypes** within the chosen segment.
2. **Focus Job** — the one job statement you're building around, for the chosen archetype.
3. **Job Steps** — the customer's process to get the job done end-to-end (their map, today, without you). Each step is a place where they measure success.
4. **Outcome Statements** — how the customer measures success at each step. Form: **direction + metric + object + context** ("minimize the time it takes to verify the fit before the event"). Draft 5–10; mark each `[ASSUMPTION]` until customers rank them.
5. **Rank with customers** (importance × current satisfaction). Important + unsatisfied = the underserved outcomes worth building for. Rankings by the founder or the AI are placeholders, labeled as such.

## Consistency checks

- Does the market definition (people + job) match the market opportunity chosen in `/where-to-play` (application + customer)? Flag mismatches.
- Do the outcome statements connect to the pains/gains in `/value-proposition-canvas`? They should be the same evidence, different lenses.
- Every job statement, step, and outcome drafted before interviews is `[ASSUMPTION]` — end with which ones the founder should rank-test with real customers, and how (`/customer-discovery`).
