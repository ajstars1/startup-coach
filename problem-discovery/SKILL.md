# Problem Discovery — Persona, Problem Statement, Empathy Map, Ecosystem & Workflow

Four tools, run together (they feed each other), all producing **labeled hypothesis drafts** to be destroyed or confirmed by real interviews. Everything drafted pre-interview is `[ASSUMPTION]` by definition.

## 1. Customer Persona (provisional, pre-discovery)

Consider 2+ candidate archetypes first; pick a primary with justification, keep the runner-up listed. Then fill:
1. **Name** (fictional, for role-playing — label it so)
2. **Description** — demographics, goals, needs
3. **Key problems/needs** your idea aims to solve
4. **Current solutions** available to them (real, findable ones)
5. **Desired outcomes** — what success looks like *for them* (must eventually come from their mouth, not yours)

Rebuild the persona from real quotes after interviews. Watch for the early-adopter tell: the person with the most acute version of the problem (per *Talking to Humans*) often isn't the "average" customer — real interviews frequently reveal a sharper archetype than the founder's initial guess.

## 2. Problem Statement — use this exact mad-lib

> **When** `<context>` **occurs,** `<customer>` **struggles with** `<problem>`**. Because of this, they feel** `<emotional response>` **and experience** `<quantifiable/practical impact>`**. Currently, they use** `<alternative solutions>` **despite** `<alternative shortcomings>`**.**

Quality bars:
- `<quantifiable impact>` must be a number with a source, or written as `[UNKNOWN — measure via interviews]`. Never invent it.
- `<alternatives>` must be real. "We have no competitors" is almost always false — customers are doing *something* today (a shop visit, a spreadsheet, a workaround, postponing). The status quo is the real competitor.
- Add: who else is affected if this continues, and **who will pay** for a solution (user ≠ buyer, often).
- Honest check: if you have negative-space evidence (a silent launch, low signups), state what it does and doesn't imply — "no felt pain" vs "offer never reached them" are different diagnoses.

## 3. Empathy Map (Dave Gray, XPLANE — [gamestorming.com](https://gamestorming.com/empathy-mapping/))

Quadrants: **Says / Thinks / Does / Feels**, plus Pains and Gains. Pre-interview: fill "Says" with *things to listen for* — never invented quotes. Post-interview: replace with verbatim customer language. Rank pains/gains WITH customers, not for them.

## 4. Ecosystem Map + Customer Workflow

- **Ecosystem map:** every entity around the customer — users, buyers, influencers, saboteurs, suppliers, channels, regulators, payment methods. Mark the critical roles in the purchase, consumption, and payment decisions. Identify the possible **saboteur** (whoever loses if you win — often the incumbent relationship).
- **Customer workflow:** the step-by-step process the customer follows TODAY, without your product — where time, money, and frustration occur, and where your solution would slot in. Draw today's workflow, not the imagined future one.
- **The bundling check:** friction that looks separable often isn't (e.g., if two errands always happen in one trip, removing one saves less than it appears). Find which steps are truly independent before valuing their removal.
- Quantify frictions as `[ASSUMPTION → measure]`: steps, trips, minutes, delay rates, rework rates.

## Output

Persona + problem statement + empathy map + ecosystem/workflow (mermaid diagrams welcome), every field labeled `[FACT/ASSUMPTION/UNKNOWN]`, ending with **the 5 highest-value interview questions this analysis raises** — the goal of these drafts is to be proven wrong cheaply. Hand those questions to `/customer-discovery`.
